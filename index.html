Project Overview

Our project is about visualizing the status of the 3 features: maternal mortality, healthcare spending, and insurance coverage, all of which would be linked and interacted using a common field ‘state’ (by state, we mean individual states of the U.S).
By generating appropriate visualizations that deliver the status of the three features using primarily d3.js, we hope to be able to deliver the general status and possible correlations between the three features to the general public.
To achieve our desired delivery, we would take advantage of linking/brushing (between the visualizations to enhance the interactiveness of individual data point across our datasets), infographic types (bar charts, US map, scatter plot, and table), as well as color coding and other aspects learned throughout COSI 116 to optimize the visual delivery.

Target Audience

As we intend to deliver a landscape of health-related features and how they might possibly be interrelated, the target audience that would be best addressed is the general public (specifically, web surfers).
It is easy to come across many infographics intended to raise awareness or pique interest in particular topics/subjects targeted for the general public browsing the web. Given the web is open for anyone and is used by people with a wide range of backgrounds and understanding on the topic that the infographics are focused on, we do not intend to encode any in-depth/professional data from biomedical or actuarial domains and the likes.
Conversely, to catch the web surfers’ attention sweeply and effectively, each visualization needs to encode data in a way that intuitively delivers the status of the features and their possible interrelatedness. This would require trimmed and cleaned up data to present a holistic picture, consistent color scheme for linking, brushing, and highlighting to prevent/minimize distractions, and yet not a cookie-cutter, cliche visualizations to stop web surfers from scrolling down/clicking away.
